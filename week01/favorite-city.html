My Favorite City

Lafia is the capital city of Nasarawa State in north-central Nigeria. It was founded in the late 18th century by Muhammadu Dunama, on the site south of Shabu village. In the late 19th century under ruler Mohamman Agwai (1881-1903) the Lafia market became an important trade centre in the Benue Valley; the town later became part of the British colonial structure in 1903. The city is multi-ethnic with inhabitants including Kanuri, Fulani, Gwandara, Alago, Migilli (Koro) and Hausa, making it “a miniature Nigeria” in one description.
